Practical guidance for putting Uganda’s ECCE Policy into action.

ECCE AskDesk Uganda helps different users understand what the Early Childhood Care and Education Policy, Implementation Standards and Implementation Guidelines mean in practice. It is built for the people who need quick, clear and source-backed guidance: parents, caregivers, ECCE proprietors, teachers, inspectors, Local Governments, civil society, partners and national-level teams.

The tool turns policy language into simple answers, action steps, responsible actors, checklists and self-checks. It is especially useful when someone needs to know where to start, what document to prepare, which office is responsible, or what the approved policy direction says.

What ECCE AskDesk does

It answers questions using approved ECCE source material, gives next steps, cites the source reference, and helps users prepare checklists and self-checks.

What ECCE AskDesk does not do

It does not issue licences, register centres, approve teachers, make inspection decisions, provide legal opinions, or receive child protection case details.

About ECCE AskDesk Uganda

ECCE AskDesk Uganda is a policy-grounded digital helpdesk designed to support the implementation of Uganda’s Early Childhood Care and Education Policy. It helps users move from reading policy documents to understanding what action is required, who is responsible, and where official guidance should be sought.

The tool demonstrates how digital assistance can support policy implementation, public awareness, compliance preparation, user guidance and stakeholder coordination in the ECCE sector. It draws from the ECCE Policy, Implementation Standards and Implementation Guidelines that have been prepared as source material for the knowledge base.

ECCE AskDesk supports understanding and preparation. Official decisions on registration, licensing, inspection, enforcement, teacher registration, legal interpretation and child protection remain with the mandated government institutions and statutory bodies.

Why the tool exists

Many ECCE actors need fast, practical guidance. The Policy, Standards and Guidelines contain the answers, but users may not always know where to look. ECCE AskDesk helps bridge that gap.

Who it serves

Parents, proprietors, teachers, caregivers, inspectors, Local Governments, CSOs, partners, MoES teams and other stakeholders involved in ECCE implementation.

How it works

The tool retrieves relevant source material and presents a structured answer with a simple explanation, policy basis, next steps, responsible actor, source and escalation note.

What users should expect

ECCE AskDesk is designed to give practical, source-backed guidance. It works best when users ask clear questions about ECCE implementation, registration, licensing, standards, inspection, parenting programmes, Local Government roles, EMIS, child safety, teacher/caregiver requirements or partner support.

Every answer is expected to help the user understand the issue, see what the approved documents say, know what to do next, identify who is responsible, and know when to seek official guidance.

What the tool gives

A simple answer, a short explanation of the policy basis, practical next steps, responsible actors, source references and an escalation note where official follow-up is required.

What users should do

Choose the role that best fits them, ask a specific question, read the source reference, follow the next steps, and contact the relevant authority where approval or official interpretation is needed.

What the tool cannot do

It cannot issue licences, register centres, approve teachers, replace inspectors, provide legal advice, receive abuse case details or make child protection decisions.

Examples of good questions

  • Who licenses pre-primary schools?
  • What is required for EMIS onboarding?
  • What should parents look for in a safe ECCE centre?
  • Can ECCE centres be inspected without prior notice?
  • What is the role of Local Governments in ECCE implementation?
  • What does the Policy say about parenting programmes?

When to seek official guidance

Seek official guidance when the issue involves registration, licensing, enforcement, inspection findings, legal interpretation, teacher registration decisions, child protection concerns, or any matter where a government office must make the final decision.

Support and escalation

ECCE AskDesk helps users understand policy guidance, but some matters require official follow-up. Use this page to identify where a user should go when the tool cannot make a decision.

Policy clarification

For national policy interpretation, implementation guidance or official clarification, refer to the Ministry of Education and Sports or the delegated ECCE technical office.

Registration and licensing

For centre registration, licensing, renewal, inspection or compliance follow-up, contact the relevant District, City or Municipal Local Government education office.

Teacher registration

For teacher registration, licensing or professional status questions, refer to the relevant teacher registration authority or the National Teacher Council process where applicable.

Child protection concerns

Do not enter child protection case details into this tool. Use authorised child protection structures such as the centre manager where safe, probation and family welfare structures, Local Government child protection officers, police child and family protection structures or other approved referral systems.

Technical support

For tool errors, broken pages, missing answers or import issues, use the Feedback page and describe what happened, which role you selected and what question you asked.

Partner or pilot interest

For pilot testing, technical review, content expansion or deployment discussions, prepare feedback on the tool’s accuracy, user experience, source coverage and institutional fit.
